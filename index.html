THREE SITUATIONS

Sound familiar?

SHEET 02 / 10

EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR · CIVIC TECH NGO

Amara

15 staff, four concurrent programmes, two junior programme officers. Amara is everywhere — reviewing every donor report, sitting in on field calls, chasing engineers for proposal inputs. The programmes are moving, but only because she is personally holding them together. She knows this is unsustainable but hasn't had time to fix it.

What she needs is a senior delivery lead who can come in, build the architecture, and run the programmes — someone her junior staff can learn from, without requiring her to manage them closely.

FOUNDER · YOUTH EMPLOYMENT ACCELERATOR

David

Four years running cohorts on founder hustle. Just won his first significant institutional grant — an 18-month, funded programme across two counties. He knows how to run an accelerator. He does not know how to run a grant. Milestone-based disbursements, a MEL framework, quarterly donor reporting: none of this is familiar territory. The first disbursement milestone is 90 days away and delivery has not properly started.

What he needs is someone to translate the grant agreement into a delivery plan, set up the systems, and walk him through the first reporting cycle — leaving him able to do it himself next time.

REGIONAL PROGRAMME DIRECTOR · INGO

Fatuma

A three-country digital governance programme, mid-implementation, has lost momentum. The original implementation lead left eight months ago. The replacement has not worked out. Two of three country teams are behind. A major donor review is six months away and there is no recovery plan.

What Fatuma needs is not a restructure — it is a fast, honest diagnosis of where the programme actually stands, a rebuilt delivery structure across the three country teams, and a credible path to the donor review.
